Places to Stay

Comfortable rooms are often available at the Southern Adventist University conference center at reasonable rates. There are also apartments available on campus, each with two bedrooms and four single beds, kitchen facilities and washers and driers. These are nice for families, or for participants who would like to share an apartment and split the cost. For exact pricing and reservations call (423) 236-7000 or email guestlodging@southern.edu.

 

There are a number of motels in the surrounding area along I-75. The nearby Exits 5, 7, and 11 all have motels. See a partial list below. We make no recommendations, but we hope the list will be a help to you as you plan your weekend.

Places to Eat

Three group meals are provided as part of the weekend. For other meals, the Village Market Deli is available on campus. or the Dining Hall, which is usually open for the noon meal during summer.  On the other side of White Oak Ridge there are many places to eat at Four Corners where Apison Pike and Ooltewah-Ringgold Road intersect, and also in nearby Ooltewah.

 

Because Southern Adventist University reflects the health-conscious attitudes of the Seventh-day Adventist Church, the campus is tobacco and alcohol free, and meals served are vegetarian. Coffee and other caffeine drinks are not generally available on campus, so coffee lovers may wish to bring their own instant and a mug.
